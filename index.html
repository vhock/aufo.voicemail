About Aufo Trials

Aufo Trials is an open research initiative that collects real-world voicemail recordings to help improve automated voice understanding systems. Participants call in, leave a short message, and contribute to a growing public dataset used for acoustic research and voice-interface development.

All submissions are voluntary and may be published under an open data license. No personal information is required to participate.

How It Works

1

Choose a Prompt

Browse the list of available recording prompts on this page or receive one by text message after signing up.

2

Call the Voicemail Line

Dial the Aufo Trials number and follow the automated instructions. You will be asked to speak your chosen prompt aloud.

3

Your Recording Is Submitted

After you hang up, your recording is securely stored and added to the dataset. That's it!

How to Participate

📞 By Phone

  1. Dial +1 (800) AUFO-TRL
  2. Listen to the welcome message and press 1 to begin.
  3. Wait for the beep, then read the prompt displayed below.
  4. Hang up when finished. Your message will be saved automatically.

💻 Online Upload

  1. Record a .wav or .mp3 file of yourself reading a prompt.
  2. Keep it under 60 seconds and at least 8 kHz sample rate.
  3. Email your file to submit@aufo.voicemail with the prompt ID in the subject line.
  4. You will receive a confirmation reply within 24 hours.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is participation anonymous?

Yes. We do not link recordings to any personal identity. Caller ID information is discarded immediately upon receipt.

How will my recording be used?

Recordings are transcribed, anonymised, and published as open data for academic and commercial voice research.

Can I withdraw my submission?

Because submissions are anonymous, we are unable to identify and delete individual recordings once they have been processed. Please only submit if you are comfortable with this.

What languages are accepted?

Currently English only. We plan to expand to additional languages in future trial rounds.

How many times can I participate?

You may submit as many recordings as you like, but please use a different prompt for each call.
