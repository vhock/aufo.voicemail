🎙️ VoiceMail Research

Support our acoustic research by leaving short voicemails from different rooms in your apartment, in which you briefly describe your surroundings. It only takes about 5 minutes — and helps us understand how room properties affect voice recordings.

📖 Introduction

Thank you for your interest in our acoustic research study! We are investigating how different rooms and their properties affect voice recordings.

As a participant, you will leave a voicemail of approximately 1 minute from each of 5 rooms in your apartment (bathroom, living room, kitchen, bedroom, and hallway), briefly describing your surroundings in each. The whole process takes about 5 minutes.

Talking for about 1 minute is the core of the experiment. Your descriptions don’t need to be perfectly accurate — just do your best. If describing the room doesn’t take up the full minute, feel free to simply talk about anything — the weather, your favorite dish, or “What would you take to an island?” — just keep speaking naturally.

Hmm, what else can I say... the weather today is really nice, sunny and about 20 degrees. I think if I could take one thing to a desert island, it would probably be a good book. Anyway, I think that's about a minute, goodbye!"

FAQ

Does it need to be completely silent while I record?

No — normal background noise is perfectly fine. You do not need to wait for silence. Just speak clearly so your voice is audible.

Do I need to know the exact dimensions of my rooms?

Not at all. A rough estimate is enough (e.g. "about 3 by 4 meters"). You don't need to measure anything — just describe what you see and estimate as best you can.

How is my data handled?

All recordings are stored anonymously and linked only to your participant ID. Your phone number and email will be deleted after your trial has been processed. The data is used exclusively for scientific research purposes.

How long does the whole process take?

Each voicemail should be about 1 minute long. With 5 rooms, the total time effort is approximately 5 minutes.

What if I forgot to mention something?

Don't worry — it's not a big issue if you forgot to state some information. Just try to cover as much as you can. There is no need to re-record.

What if I messed up a recording?

Simply call again and leave a new voicemail for that room. If there are multiple voicemails for the same room, only the most recent one will be used.
