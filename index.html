🎙️ VoiceMail Research

Support our acoustic research by leaving short voicemails from various rooms in your apartment and surrounding areas, briefly describing your surroundings. It only takes about 5–10 minutes and helps us understand how room acoustics affect phone call audio.

📖 Introduction

Thank you for your interest in our acoustic research study! Have you ever been on a phone call with a friend where you could hear, solely from the sound, that they were calling from a bathroom or from a railway station? That is due to the characteristic reverberation many rooms have and which we have learned to recognise. We are investigating how much of this reverberation is actually transmitted across various mobile phone models during phone calls.

As a participant, you will leave a voicemail of approximately 1-2 minutes from as many rooms as possible in your apartment and the surrounding areas (such as the bathroom, living room, kitchen, bedroom, hallway, balcony, stairway or even inside your car), briefly describing your surroundings in addition to performing small changes like opening or closing doors and windows. The whole process should take no longer than 5 to 10 minutes.

Talking for about 1-2 minutes per room is the core of the experiment. Your descriptions don't need to be perfectly accurate, just do your best. If describing the room and its sound doesn't take up the full time, feel free to simply talk about anything - the weather, your favorite dish, or just read aloud from this page - just keep speaking naturally.

💬 Example Voicemail Message

🛁 Example 1 - Bathroom

"Hello, this is a participant. I am in the bathroom. The door is closed. The bathroom is approximately 4 meters wide and 3 meters across, ceiling height is approximately 2.5 meters. The floor is tiled, and the walls are tiled up to a height of approx 2 meters. The rest is wallpaper. There is a sink, a toilet and a bathtub. I am walking around the room, now I am standing rather close to a wall - it sounds quite reverberant near the tiles. Okay, I just opened the door - I am walking around again, and the sound feels noticeably more open now, less echoey. There is also a small window above the bathtub; I just opened the window as well, and now the sound is even drier and a bit of street noise comes in. Hmm, what else can I say... the weather today is really nice, sunny and about 20 degrees. I think if I could take one thing to a desert island, it would probably be a good book. Anyway, I think that's about a minute, goodbye!"

🛏️ Example 2 - Bedroom

"Hi, this is a participant. I am in my bedroom. The door is closed. The bedroom is roughly 4 by 3.5 meters, with a ceiling height of about 2.6 meters. The floor is hardwood with a large rug in the middle, the walls are painted plaster, and the ceiling is also painted. There is a queen-size bed against the long wall, two nightstands with lamps, a tall wardrobe opposite the bed, a small desk with a chair near the window, and curtains on the window. There are also a few framed pictures on the walls and a houseplant in the corner. I am walking around the room while talking - near the wardrobe it sounds a tiny bit warmer, and standing on the rug the sound feels a bit softer than near the wall. Okay, I just opened the window - I am walking around the room again, and honestly, it sounds pretty much the same to me, maybe just a little more outside noise. That's about it for the bedroom, talk to you next time, bye!"

  • You choose which rooms to record - you do not need to cover them all. The list below is just a suggestion; feel free to add your own places, such as an attic, storage room, basement, garage, or anywhere else interesting:
    • 🛁 Bathroom

    • 🚪 Hallway
    • 🪴 Balcony

  • For each call, go into the room and close the door.
  • Start by introducing yourself as a participant (e.g. "Hello, this is a participant").
  • State which room you are in (e.g. "I am in the bathroom").
  • Estimate and mention the size of the room (e.g. "approximately 3 by 4 meters, ceiling height about 2.5 meters"). A rough estimate is fine.
  • Describe the surface materials - floors, walls, and ceiling (e.g. tiles, carpet, hardwood, concrete, wallpaper).
  • Describe any other features you notice - furniture, windows, curtains, appliances, etc.
  • After describing the room, walk around and listen for yourself - do you notice spots where the acoustics change? Try standing close to a wall, in a corner, in the middle of the room, or next to a hard surface, and narrate what you are doing and what you hear (e.g. "I'm walking to the wall now - it sounds different here", "in the corner it feels narrower"). Then, if the room has doors or windows, open or close them, say out loud what you did (e.g. "I just opened the window", "I just closed the door"), and walk around the room again while describing how it sounds now. Don't worry if you can't put the change into acoustic terms - saying "it sounds the same" or "it sounds different" is perfectly fine.
  • Talking for about 1-2 minutes is the core of the experiment. If your room description doesn't fill the full time, just keep talking about anything - the weather, your favorite dish, or simply read aloud from this page. The important thing is to keep speaking naturally.

FAQ

Does it need to be completely silent while I record?

No - normal background noise is perfectly fine. You do not need to wait for silence. Just speak clearly so your voice is audible.

Do I need to know the exact dimensions of my rooms?

Not at all. A rough estimate is enough (e.g. "about 3 by 4 meters"). You don't need to measure anything - just describe what you see and estimate as best you can.

How is my data handled?

All recordings are stored anonymously and linked only to your participant ID. Your phone number and email will be deleted after your trial has been processed. The data is used exclusively for scientific research purposes. If you would like us to delete your data sooner, please use the data deletion form at the bottom of this page.

How long does the whole process take?

Each voicemail should be about 1-2 minutes long. Depending on how many rooms you record, the total time is approximately 5 to 10 minutes.

What if I forgot to mention something?

Don't worry - it's not a big issue if you forgot to state some information. Just try to cover as much as you can. There is no need to re-record.

What if I messed up a recording?

If you are not happy with your voicemail, simply stay on the line until about 2 minutes 30 seconds - you will then be prompted whether you would like to delete the message and re-record it. Alternatively, you can just call again and leave a new voicemail for that room; if there are multiple voicemails for the same room, only the most recent one will be used.

Which languages can I leave the voicemail in?

English and German are both perfectly fine.
